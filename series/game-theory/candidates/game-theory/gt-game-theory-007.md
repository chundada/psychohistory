## 原文引用 (English)
"Control is more important than dominance. Okay? Control is more important than dominance. Why? Because control is the idea of calibration. Calibration means that you time or you structure or you strategize your response in a way that helps you achieve your strategic objective. You're not just going to punch, you're you're throwing a punch in a certain way that allows you to defend yourself, that strikes fear in the opponent, and also allows you to seem as the good guy among spectators. Okay? And then when the police come, you can justify why you threw that punch in a certain way to the police so that you don't go to jail."

"Another way of saying this is that calibration is ultimately about strategic flexibility. And the idea of strategic flexibility is in a fight, the person who has the most options, the person who um has the most flexible strategy will usually win the fight."

"By calibrating your movements strategically, you can manipulate the bully into self-destruction."

## 中文解读
这是该系列中最重要的战略博弈框架之一。讲者通过"学校恶霸"的比喻，颠覆了传统的"升级优势"（escalation dominance）理论——即谁有更强的武力谁就赢。他提出"控制优于压倒"：真正的优势不在于有多少武器（nukes, guns），而在于能否校准（calibrate）自己的反应，保持战略灵活性。恶霸为了维护"信誉"（credibility）被迫升级，而挑战者可以选择何时、如何、是否回应，从而操纵恶霸走向自我毁灭。
