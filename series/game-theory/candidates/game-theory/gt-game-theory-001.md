There are the players, the participants. Okay? Then there are the rules or the constraints. Um in mathematics we would call this the boundary conditions, okay? The limits to the game. And the third aspect are the incentives. Basically, how do you win this game? What do you get from this game? And my argument to you this semester is that if you study all three, okay? If you got who the If you figure out who the players are, if you got who the If you understand the rules, you understand the incentives, then you understand how the game works and then therefore you can predict how the game will turn out."

"We call this The word in game theory we call we use for this is called the Nash equilibrium. Okay? Nash equilibrium just means that you always reach a state in which all the players maximize their outcome, the Nash equilibrium. And so, the world makes sense now. There's a problem with this, though. The problem is in real life, no one does this. Okay? In real life, no one actually follows this rule. Nash equilibrium is just a theory we made up. ## 中文解读
这是整个Game Theory系列的基础框架。讲者将博弈论简化为三个核心要素：玩家（Players）、规则/约束条件（Rules）和激励/收益（Incentives）。他提出，一旦理解了这三个要素，就能预测游戏的走向。同时他指出，经典纳什均衡假设玩家追求利益最大化，但在现实生活中，人们实际上追求的不是"最优"而是"地位"（status），后者是零和博弈，因此现实中的纳什均衡往往被打破。
