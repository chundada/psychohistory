"So, each country I want you to imagine each country as a chess set. Okay. So this is a chess set and I want to show you um how each player behaves using this chess set as as a template as a frame frame of reference. Okay. So the king is the political system. All right. So the first idea to understand is that when you fight a war, you're not trying to defeat the other person's military. Doesn't really get you anywhere. What you're really trying to do is you're trying to put your opponent in check by attacking the political system. The queen is the grand strategy. The grand strategy is again how each player perceives geopolitics and how to best dominate geopolitics. And then you have the bishop, the knight and the rook. And this are the attack vectors. Your mechanisms, your tools for achieving your grand strategy. And now you have the pawn. And the pawn are just that.
