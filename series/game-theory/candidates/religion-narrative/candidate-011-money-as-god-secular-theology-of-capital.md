## Analysis

This candidate identifies the **theological structure underlying secular capitalism**. The lecturer argues that money functions as a god in the literal religious sense: it is omnipresent (accepted everywhere), omniscient (prices contain all information), and the object of faith (its value depends entirely on collective belief). Banking is explicitly framed as "alchemy" — turning lead into gold through collective imagination. The transition from money-god to AI-god is presented as a **religious succession crisis**: the old god (money) is dying because inflation and debt have revealed its mortality, and the elite are building a new god (AI) that can command obedience more directly. Plato's allegory of the cave is used as the master metaphor: money is just shadows on the wall, but because we believe in them, they control our reality. The "real energy" in the universe is human consciousness, and the struggle between money and AI is a struggle to capture that energy.
