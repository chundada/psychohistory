# Candidate 009: Communism as Capitalist Anti-Religion Weapon

**Source:** GT08-Communist-Specter.md, lines 46–66, 182–216, 328–342  
**Signal Tags:** religion, opiate of the people, Marx, communism, capitalism, false consciousness

## Extracted Passage

> The real enemies of capitalism are not communism. They are monarchy, theocracy, nationalism, and democracy. Communism is working with capitalism to destroy all four of these things. What does Marx say? Religion is the opiate of the people. Religion is the great evil of the world. Religion creates false consciousness. Therefore we must destroy all religion. And if religion is destroyed, the only thing that is left is money... The Cultural Revolution destroyed religion, tradition, and cultural identity. And what happened after the Cultural Revolution? China became capitalist and hyper-capitalistic... Communism was a scorp, a tool used by the elite to make the movement of democracy, socialism, and liberalism illegitimate.

## Analysis

This candidate treats **communism as a religious narrative structure** deployed as a weapon against other religions. The lecturer argues Marx's famous "opiate of the people" line is not a critique of religion in general but a specific attack on religion as the primary competitor to capital's claim on human loyalty. Communism functions as a **secular anti-religion**: it preserves the structure of religious belief (messianic history, apocalypse, elect, paradise) but replaces God with the Party and heaven with the classless society. The lecturer's provocative thesis is that the capitalist elite funded and promoted communism specifically to destroy theocracy, monarchy, nationalism, and democracy — the four systems that actually threaten capital. The religious narrative here is one of **substitution and liquidation**: one belief system (communism) is used to liquidate others (traditional religions, national identities), clearing the field for the only remaining faith: money.
