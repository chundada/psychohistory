## Analysis

This is the most comprehensive mapping of **cross-religious narrative convergence** in the entire series. The lecturer identifies seven distinct eschatological traditions and demonstrates that despite their surface differences, they all predict (or require) the same set of geopolitical events: a war in the Middle East, the destruction of the Al-Aqsa Mosque, the building of the Third Temple, a final world war, and the emergence of a one-world government. This convergence is presented not as divine prophecy but as a **predictive model**: because these narratives are the operating systems of the societies involved, the actors will behave in ways that make these outcomes more likely. The Law of Eschatological Convergence is explicitly positioned as a game-theoretic tool for forecasting geopolitics.
